The making of "Safhaa"

:بسم الله الرحمان الرحيم

لنصنع "صفحة"  

"Safhaa" is an Islamic feed for Chrome's new tab. the premise is simple.

The new tab will serve as a place to drag and drop and organize different "components". 
The new tab will become a place for you to improve your spirituality as well as productivity. 
So there are two main parts to the app:
1-Spiritual Features
2- Productivity Features

In this version, we will start with these components:

Islamic Features:


- The time and date:

(both in Hijri and Georgian calendars)
Ability to switch the primary date format. Either display the hijri as a main calendar or the Georgian.

 

Another format is the compact Time+Date variation.

 

- Prayer Times:


When the prayer time is close ( less than 10 minutes away ), the indicator turns red to subtly bring attention to the user that they should start preparing themselves for prayer.


- Time Remaining for the next prayer. (two layouts for remaining prayer time)


-Wisdom.


The user can view quotes from the Quran and Hadith. The user should scroll until the bar on the right becomes filled to view the next Ayah or Hadith.
In the next version, we can add a button that takes the user to (quran.com) so they can continue reading from there and read the explanation as well. More awesome illustrations will be added إن شاء الله.


- Reminders of the next important Islamic event

such as fasting specific dates (Achoura'a or Monday and Thursday) or a reminder of certain important dates such as the new Hijri year.


- Show one of Allah's 99 Names.

The purpose is to familiarize with them and to eventually memorize them and know their meaning. Clicking the element opens a link explaining the name in [ islamweb.com ] [ look for an article link for every name on Islamweb ]

 

Productivity Features:


- Weather quick glance.


- A quick way to take notes and to-dos.

- White noise (sharia-compliant)


-Quran radio:

I noticed that unless I want to hear or memorize a specific Surah of the Quran, I want to just click and start listening without having to spend time choosing what to listen to. sometimes giving up control is better. Just click on the start button and you start listening right away.

 

- Focus timer simpler Pomodoro technique.

I have always liked focused sprints. however, I find that I only use them to make me aware of the time I am spending, not to take a break.
Usually, when I use the Pomodoro technique, I just get distracted when the timer goes and "reminds me to take a break". 
I work in 15-minute sprints, and that keeps me focused on the task at hand, if the timer goes off and I find myself distracted, I get back to the main thing. I take breaks when I feel like I need it, not systematically. we are not robots.
